Article on suicide:
Depression the risk factor

     Suicide is the act of intentionally causing one's own death. 
Recent statistics from the World health organisation (WHO) shows that no fewer than a million people die annually from suicide. There are an estimated 10 to 20 million attempted suicides every year.

There is no single reason why someone may try to take his or her own life. About 90 percent of people who commit suicide have a mental illness at the time of their death. several mental disorders like bipolar disorder, schizophrenia and depression can contribute to suicide but depression is the risk factor.
Depression is a common and serious mental illness that negatively affects how you feel, think and act. It causes a feeling of sadness and/or loss of interest in activities once enjoyed. It can lead to a variety of emotional and physical problems. Depression can strike at any time, it can affect anyone, even a person who appears to live in a relatively ideal circumstances. According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), depression is the most common illness worldwide and the leading causes of disability. They estimate that 350 million people are affected by depression, globally. 

     Continues exposure to violence, neglect, abuse or poverty may make some people more vulnerable to depression. There are many symptoms of depression but the worst symptom is the thought of death or suicide.
Depression is a treatable mental illness, ranging from antidepressants to psychotherapy. 
Self-help tips to deal with depression

* Challenge negative thinking and thoughts of suicide or death

* Do things that make you feel good, even when you don't feel like

* Reach out and stay connected to supportive people
